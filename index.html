Minxing Zhang 张敏行

Ph.D. Student  ·  Computer Science  ·  Duke University

I am a third-year Ph.D. student in Computer Science at Duke University, advised by Dr. Jian Pei. Before joining Duke, I completed my undergraduate studies at Emory University, where I was advised by Dr. Li Xiong and Dr. Liang Zhao.

My research focuses on trustworthiness in AI models — particularly privacy and generalizability in large language models and LLM-based agents — with applications in data markets and spatio-temporal data mining. I am currently exploring LLMs and LLM-based agents from a conversation-level perspective, including high-quality conversation generation and evaluation, as well as privacy-preserving model performance estimation.

I am open to research collaborations and internship opportunities. I have always believed that words are the gentlest way to connect — perhaps we are in different cities, looking at different skies, living entirely different lives, but through a single message, we can briefly step into each other's world. Feel free to reach out. I hope we can be friends.
